1 ## api_cbor_event_courier — fire-and-forget ring for CBOR FFI event delivery.
2 ## ============================================================================
3 ## Part D-3 of the CBOR refactoring (doc/CBOR_Round2_PartD_EventCourier.md).
4 ##
5 ## A CBOR-mode event emitted by a provider on the processing thread needs to
6 ## fan out to all foreign-callback subscribers without blocking the provider.
7 ## The shape is the mirror image of `api_cbor_courier`:
8 ##
9 ## producer (processing thread)
10 ## 1. CBOR-encode the event payload **once** into a shared-heap buffer,
11 ## 2. enqueue an `EventMsg` carrying `(eventName, ctx, buf, bufLen)`
12 ## — ownership of `buf` transfers to the consumer,
13 ## 3. wake the delivery thread via its broker dispatch signal.
14 ##
15 ## consumer (delivery thread, via `registerBrokerPoller`)
16 ## 1. dequeue messages from the ring,
17 ## 2. snapshot the foreign-subscriber list for `(ctx, eventName)`,
18 ## 3. invoke each foreign callback synchronously,
19 ## 4. free the buffer.
20 ##
21 ## Differences from `api_cbor_courier`:
22 ## - **No response slots, no `inFlight` counter** — events are
23 ## fire-and-forget. Producers do not block, do not wait for a reply.
24 ## - Ring is sized for **burst capacity** (default 256), not for
25 ## concurrent in-flight count. A full ring drops the event with a
26 ## diagnostic (logged by the caller) — appropriate for the
27 ## fire-and-forget contract.
28 ## - `eventName` is carried inline as a fixed-size NUL-terminated
29 ## ASCII buffer (same convention as `CborCallMsg.apiName`) so the
30 ## message stays POD — zero GC involvement on the producer side.
31 ##
32 ## This module is plain runtime code (NOT codegen) used by the generated
33 ## library runtime in `api_library.nim`.
