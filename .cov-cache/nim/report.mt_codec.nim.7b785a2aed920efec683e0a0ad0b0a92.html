1 ## Runtime marshal / unmarshal helpers for (mt) broker payloads.
2 ##
3 ## The broker macro emits two thin per-type wrappers
4 ## (`<TypeName>MtMarshal` / `<TypeName>MtUnmarshal`) that call into the
5 ## generic `mtMarshalValue` / `mtUnmarshalValue` defined here. Those
6 ## generics use `when supportsCopyMem(T):` + Nim's `fieldPairs` to walk
7 ## arbitrary payload types at compile time, recursing into:
8 ##
9 ## - **POD types** (scalars, enums, distinct-of-POD, fixed POD arrays,
10 ## objects whose fields are all POD): single `copyMem(sizeof(T))`.
11 ## `supportsCopyMem` correctly classifies all of these.
12 ## - **`string`**: 4-byte little-endian length + bytes.
13 ## - **`seq[U]`**: 4-byte length + per-element recursive marshal.
14 ## - **`array[N, U]` where U is non-POD**: per-element recursive marshal.
15 ## - **objects with non-POD fields**: `fieldPairs` walks each field
16 ## recursively.
17 ##
18 ## Forbidden (caught at the call site by a compile-time `{.error.}`):
19 ## `ref T`, `ptr T`, `pointer`, `cstring`, proc-typed fields.
20 ##
21 ## Strings and seqs allocate on the *consumer thread's GC heap* during
22 ## unmarshal — no thread-local pointer ever crosses a broker boundary,
23 ## which is the §2.6 fix in practice.
