1 ## BrokerInterface — an abstract, OOP-style facade over a group of Event /
2 ## Request brokers (see doc/HIERARCHICAL_BROKERS_PLAN.md, phase P3).
3 ##
4 ## A `BrokerInterface` block declares the *contract*: the events it can emit
5 ## and the requests it answers. It generates:
6 ## * a `ref object of RootObj` interface type carrying a hidden `brokerCtx`;
7 ## * the underlying Event/Request brokers (re-emitted verbatim, or lowered to
8 ## their `(API)` variants when the interface is declared `(API)`);
9 ## * one abstract `{.base.}` `method` per request (pure-virtual — raises
10 ## until a `BrokerImplement` derived type overrides it);
11 ## * a generic instance-scoped event facade (`self.emit` / `self.listen` /
12 ## `self.dropListener`) that injects `self.brokerCtx`.
13 ##
14 ## Invocation forms (note: `BrokerInterface(API) IFace:` does NOT parse in Nim —
15 ## the `(API)` binds as a call; use the comma form instead):
16 ## BrokerInterface IFace: ## or BrokerInterface(IFace):
17 ## EventBroker: ...
18 ## RequestBroker: ...
19 ## BrokerInterface(API, IFace): ## (API) propagates to every sub-broker
20 ## EventBroker: ...
21 ## RequestBroker: ...
22 ##
23 ## Requests inside an interface use the proc-sugar form (a lowercase verb proc);
24 ## the verb becomes the abstract method name a `BrokerImplement` overrides.
