1 ## api_cbor_tuple
2 ## ---------------
3 ## Map-shaped CBOR encoders/decoders for named Nim tuple aliases used
4 ## across the FFI boundary.
5 ##
6 ## ## Why this exists
7 ##
8 ## `cbor_serialization` 0.3.0 emits Nim tuples as positional CBOR arrays
9 ## (`writer.nim:423` — `proc write*[T: tuple]`) and decodes them
10 ## symmetrically (`reader_impl.nim:144` — `proc read*[T: tuple]`).
11 ## Wrapper-side codegen (Cpp / Py / Rust / Go) emits a tuple alias as
12 ## a struct with NAMED fields and expects a CBOR map keyed by those
13 ## names. Without alignment, a wrapper round-trip of `seq[TupleRow]`
14 ## fails with "invalid type: sequence, expected map".
15 ##
16 ## This module provides a macro `bindCborTupleMap(T)` that emits a
17 ## per-tuple `write` / `read` overload bound to the `BrokerCbor` flavor.
18 ## The overloads encode/consume a CBOR map keyed by the Nim field
19 ## names. Resolver code calls the macro for every named tuple alias
20 ## that's auto-registered as part of the FFI surface.
21 ##
22 ## ## Limitation
23 ##
24 ## Only NAMED tuple aliases are supported (e.g.
25 ## `type TupleRow = tuple[key: string, payload: string]`). Unnamed
26 ## positional tuples (`tuple[int32, string]`) keep the library default
27 ## (positional CBOR array) — wrappers receive synthesised field names
28 ## (`first`, `second`, ...) on the struct side which would not match
29 ## a positional CBOR shape; the tuple-as-struct codegen rejects > 9
30 ## positional elements anyway, so no wrapper currently emits structs
31 ## from unnamed tuples.
